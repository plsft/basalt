# ProductHunt — listing

**Name:** Basalt
**Tagline:** A second-brain compiler. Compiles you, not the corpus.

**Description (one-liner shown in feed):**
Basalt reads your Markdown vault and tells you what you're really working on.
Five verbs, one weekly Brief, local-first. Open-source CLI + plugin + MCP +
desktop + hosted cockpit.

**Long description:**
Most second-brain tools index your notes so you can search them. Basalt
indexes the *structure* of your vault and surfaces what you believe but
never explicitly wrote down — the through-line, the contradictions, the drift,
the buried insight from years ago that your current work keeps mining.

Five verbs map to five elements: Sodium (your implicit thesis), Chlorine
(where you contradict yourself), Mercury (which projects drifted), Carbon
(notes that are secretly the same idea), Gold (the line from 2022 you keep
re-discovering).

Open tier is free and MIT-licensed. Pro is $12/mo for hosted briefs + E2EE
vault sync. First 200 Founders get $240 lifetime Pro.

**Maker comment:**
Hey ProductHunt — I built Basalt because my own 2,400-note vault grew past
the point I could hold it in my head, and I didn't trust retrieval to tell
me what I actually thought. I wanted a one-page artifact, in my voice, that I
could read on a Sunday morning and feel oriented. So I built one.

The engine is open-source. The five surfaces (CLI, Obsidian plugin, MCP
server, native desktop, web cockpit) all run from the same core. Read-only on
your vault, no telemetry, no chat-with-your-notes.

Happy to answer anything. — George
