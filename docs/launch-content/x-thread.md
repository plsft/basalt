# X thread — launch day

**1/**
Shipped: Basalt — a second-brain compiler that reads your Markdown notes and
tells you what you're really working on.

Five verbs. One Brief, weekly. Local-first. MIT.

basalt.dev

**2/**
Not search. Not chat-with-your-notes. Basalt *compiles* your vault — the
link graph, the embeddings, the recency — and surfaces what you already
believe but never wrote down.

**3/**
Five elements, five questions about your own work:

— Sodium (Na): your implicit thesis
— Chlorine (Cl): where you're disagreeing with yourself
— Mercury (Hg): projects whose meaning has drifted
— Carbon (C): notes that are secretly the same idea
— Gold (Au): the line from 2022 your current work keeps mining

**4/**
Three things Basalt does not do:

1) Phone home (Open tier — no network at all)
2) Modify your notes (read-only on the vault, by architectural test)
3) Impose a methodology (no Zettelkasten religion required)

**5/**
The wedge: structural reasoning across your corpus, not retrieval from it.

If I search "what do I think about agents," I get notes I already remember
writing. If I run `basalt brief`, I get a paragraph about a thesis I've been
circling for 8 months without naming.

**6/**
Built in TypeScript. Runs on Bun. Single engine across:
— CLI (`npm i -g basalted`)
— Obsidian plugin
— MCP server (works with Claude Desktop, Cursor)
— Native desktop (Tauri 2, macOS/Linux/Windows)
— Web cockpit (Pro)

**7/**
Open tier: free, MIT-licensed, forever.
Pro: $12/mo for hosted briefs + E2EE vault sync.
Founder: $240 one-time, lifetime Pro. First 200.

**8/**
Why? I have a 2,400-note vault. I can't hold the whole shape in my head
anymore. I don't want chat. I want a one-page artifact in my voice that
shows me what I've been circling.

Basalt is that artifact, repeatable, weekly.
