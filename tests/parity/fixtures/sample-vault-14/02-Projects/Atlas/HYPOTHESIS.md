# Atlas — Original Hypothesis

> **The conviction that won't go away:** the work that compounds is the work nobody asked for.

## Context

Every meaningful project I've shipped started this way. The market doesn't reward what it knows it wants — it rewards what it discovers it needed. Atlas is a side-bet on this exact pattern: a tool I want to use that nobody on the team has requested, that pulls together threads from three older projects nobody said belonged together.

## The claim

The asked-for work is bounded by the asker's imagination. The unasked work is bounded only by attention. Over a long enough window, attention compounds and asks-for-X drains.

> [!tip] The shape of the bet
> If a year from now Atlas is the most-used tool internally, the thesis is that meaningful adoption follows unrequested ergonomics, not requested features. If Atlas dies in six weeks of underuse, the thesis is that "the work nobody asked for" is the same as "the work nobody needs."

## Why this matters

Most of the SignalBot work was a response to existing market structure — important, well-scoped, but inherently bounded by what the market revealed it wanted. Atlas is the opposite shape. The compounding work runs ahead of stated demand.
