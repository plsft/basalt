What I keep noticing in my own work is you can't fix a culture problem with tooling, but the wrong tooling rots a healthy one. If you take the long view, the moat isn't speed alone — it's the user's willingness to keep coming back.

> [!note] Worth pinning
> the cheapest correction happens when the constraint is still legible.

The bit I keep forgetting and re-deriving is the right abstraction is the one you can throw away in six months without panic. Actually, I was wrong about that.
