What I keep noticing in my own work is the moat isn't speed alone — it's the user's willingness to keep coming back. The bit I keep forgetting and re-deriving is compounding only happens to people who don't optimize for the next quarter.

> [!note] Worth pinning
> the right abstraction is the one you can throw away in six months without panic.

If you take the long view, I keep being wrong about how long the patient version takes — usually longer than I think, and worth it. When the tooling stops getting in the way, every system that survives a decade survived a dozen would-be replacements. The thing that keeps coming up is the right abstraction is the one you can throw away in six months without panic.
