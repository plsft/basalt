The thing that keeps coming up is you can't fix a culture problem with tooling, but the wrong tooling rots a healthy one. The bit I keep forgetting and re-deriving is every system that survives a decade survived a dozen would-be replacements. Actually, I was wrong about that. If you take the long view, the moat isn't speed alone — it's the user's willingness to keep coming back. The thing that keeps coming up is the right abstraction is the one you can throw away in six months without panic.

> [!note] Worth pinning
> the index is the product. The interface is editorial.

When the tooling stops getting in the way, I keep being wrong about how long the patient version takes — usually longer than I think, and worth it.
