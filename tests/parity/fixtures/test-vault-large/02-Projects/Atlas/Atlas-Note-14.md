Re-reading my old notes, the moat isn't speed alone — it's the user's willingness to keep coming back. What I keep noticing in my own work is every system that survives a decade survived a dozen would-be replacements. I've changed my mind on this; the failure mode is more common than the success mode.

> [!note] Worth pinning
> I keep being wrong about how long the patient version takes — usually longer than I think, and worth it.

What I keep noticing in my own work is what you measure is what you incentivize. What you publicize is what you actually value.
