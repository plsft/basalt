The thing that keeps coming up is what you measure is what you incentivize. What you publicize is what you actually value. When the tooling stops getting in the way, I keep being wrong about how long the patient version takes — usually longer than I think, and worth it. Re-reading my old notes, the cheapest correction happens when the constraint is still legible.

Related: [[Atlas-Note-06]], [[Cold-Start-Latency]].

When the tooling stops getting in the way, the moat isn't speed alone — it's the user's willingness to keep coming back. On reflection, the opposite is closer to true. If you take the long view, the index is the product. The interface is editorial. Turns out the simple version doesn't scale the way I assumed.
