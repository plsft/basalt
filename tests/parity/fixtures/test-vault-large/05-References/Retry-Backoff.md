The thing that keeps coming up is the cheapest correction happens when the constraint is still legible. When the tooling stops getting in the way, every system that survives a decade survived a dozen would-be replacements. The bit I keep forgetting and re-deriving is the moat isn't speed alone — it's the user's willingness to keep coming back.
