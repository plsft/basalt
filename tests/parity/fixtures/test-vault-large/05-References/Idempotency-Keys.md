The thing that keeps coming up is I keep being wrong about how long the patient version takes — usually longer than I think, and worth it. If you take the long view, every system that survives a decade survived a dozen would-be replacements.

Related: [[Beacon-Note-03]], [[Stigler-1971]].

If you take the long view, the moat isn't speed alone — it's the user's willingness to keep coming back. The bit I keep forgetting and re-deriving is the cheapest correction happens when the constraint is still legible.
