What I keep noticing in my own work is the moat isn't speed alone — it's the user's willingness to keep coming back. Re-reading my old notes, you can't fix a culture problem with tooling, but the wrong tooling rots a healthy one. I've changed my mind on this; the failure mode is more common than the success mode. If you take the long view, the moat isn't speed alone — it's the user's willingness to keep coming back. On reflection, the opposite is closer to true.
