Re-reading my old notes, the cheapest correction happens when the constraint is still legible. Turns out the simple version doesn't scale the way I assumed. What I keep noticing in my own work is the moat isn't speed alone — it's the user's willingness to keep coming back. On reflection, the opposite is closer to true. When the tooling stops getting in the way, every system that survives a decade survived a dozen would-be replacements.

> [!note] Worth pinning
> the right abstraction is the one you can throw away in six months without panic.
