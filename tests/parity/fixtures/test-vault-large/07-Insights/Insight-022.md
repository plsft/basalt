The thing that keeps coming up is trust is not a metric. It's a side effect of doing the boring thing for years. Re-reading my old notes, every system that survives a decade survived a dozen would-be replacements. On reflection, the opposite is closer to true. The thing that keeps coming up is the cheapest correction happens when the constraint is still legible. Turns out the simple version doesn't scale the way I assumed. When the tooling stops getting in the way, trust is not a metric. It's a side effect of doing the boring thing for years.

> [!note] Worth pinning
> compounding only happens to people who don't optimize for the next quarter.
