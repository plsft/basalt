If you take the long view, the moat isn't speed alone — it's the user's willingness to keep coming back. I've changed my mind on this; the failure mode is more common than the success mode. The bit I keep forgetting and re-deriving is trust is not a metric. It's a side effect of doing the boring thing for years.

> [!note] Worth pinning

What I keep noticing in my own work is the index is the product. The interface is editorial. If you take the long view, the right abstraction is the one you can throw away in six months without panic. Re-reading my old notes, the cheapest correction happens when the constraint is still legible.
