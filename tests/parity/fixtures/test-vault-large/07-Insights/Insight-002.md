Re-reading my old notes, compounding only happens to people who don't optimize for the next quarter. If you take the long view, the moat isn't speed alone — it's the user's willingness to keep coming back.

> [!note] Worth pinning
> the cheapest correction happens when the constraint is still legible.

The bit I keep forgetting and re-deriving is you can't fix a culture problem with tooling, but the wrong tooling rots a healthy one. What I keep noticing in my own work is trust is not a metric. It's a side effect of doing the boring thing for years.
