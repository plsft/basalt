Re-reading my old notes, the moat isn't speed alone — it's the user's willingness to keep coming back. The thing that keeps coming up is the cheapest correction happens when the constraint is still legible.

> [!note] Worth pinning
> I keep being wrong about how long the patient version takes — usually longer than I think, and worth it.

What I keep noticing in my own work is trust is not a metric. It's a side effect of doing the boring thing for years. The bit I keep forgetting and re-deriving is you can't fix a culture problem with tooling, but the wrong tooling rots a healthy one. Turns out the simple version doesn't scale the way I assumed.
