Re-reading my old notes, what you measure is what you incentivize. What you publicize is what you actually value. When the tooling stops getting in the way, the index is the product. The interface is editorial. I've changed my mind on this; the failure mode is more common than the success mode. When the tooling stops getting in the way, trust is not a metric. It's a side effect of doing the boring thing for years.

> [!note] Worth pinning
> compounding only happens to people who don't optimize for the next quarter.

Re-reading my old notes, every system that survives a decade survived a dozen would-be replacements. Actually, I was wrong about that. If you take the long view, the right abstraction is the one you can throw away in six months without panic.
