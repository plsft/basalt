When the tooling stops getting in the way, the right abstraction is the one you can throw away in six months without panic. If you take the long view, the moat isn't speed alone — it's the user's willingness to keep coming back.

> [!note] Worth pinning
> compounding only happens to people who don't optimize for the next quarter.

What I keep noticing in my own work is the cheapest correction happens when the constraint is still legible.
